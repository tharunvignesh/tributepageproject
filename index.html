Dr.APJ Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Indian president, Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, talks with school students at Rashtrapati Bhavan, presidential palace, on the eve of India's Republic Day, 25 January, 2007.

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born in 15 October 1931.Kalam started working at a very young age since he came from a very poor background.It is even said that to support his family, he used to distribute newspapers after school.He did not score good grades but was described as a bright and hardworking student.It is also said that he always had a strong desire to learn.

Abdul Kalam graduated in Physics in the year 1954.At Defense Research and Development Organisation (DRDO), Kalam designed a small helicopter for the Indian Army.Kalam was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in the year 1969 where he was the project Director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III). The vehicle was used to deploy the Rohini satellite in the earth's orbit in 1980.Kalam was also invited by Raja Ramanna to witness the country's first nuclear test, Smiling Buddham as the representative of Terminal Ballistics Research Laboratory.He served as the the 11th President of India from the year 2002 to 2007.

In 2009, he became the first Asian to be honoured with the Hoover Medal which is America's top engineering prize.He has also received honorary doctorates from more than 30 universities.The Government of India has honoured him with the Padma Bhushan in 1981 and the Padma Vibhushan in 1990.He was also honoured with the Bharat Ratna 1997.

"We will be remembered only if we give to our younger generation a prosperous and safe India, resulting out of economic prosperity coupled with civilizational heritage" - DR.APJ ABDUL KALAM
